# H-EE-56: Arrow of Time = R(n) Irreversibility

## Hypothesis

> R(n) = sigma(n)*phi(n)/(n*tau(n)) encodes the arrow of time. R < 1 means the system
> is irreversible — entropy increases, time flows forward. R = 1 means reversible — no
> net entropy production, thermodynamic time stops. The asymmetry of time is the
> asymmetry of R away from 1.

## Background

- Second law of thermodynamics: entropy never decreases in isolated systems
- Reversible processes: dS = 0, require infinite time
- Irreversible processes: dS > 0, occur in finite time — time has direction
- R(n) for most n < 6: sigma*phi < n*tau → R < 1 → irreversible regime
- R(6) = 1: unique fixed point where the system is thermodynamically balanced
- Landauer's principle: irreversible computation costs kT*ln(2) per bit erased
- R < 1 systems must dissipate energy — they are inherently time-asymmetric
- At R = 1 (n = 6), computation is thermodynamically reversible — time is symmetric

## Predictions

1. Physical processes exhibiting R = 1 ratios should show reduced entropy production
2. The irreversibility rate of a process scales as (1 - R)^2 (distance from balanced point)
3. At cosmological scales, the universe's R-score drifted from ~1 at the Big Bang
4. Biological systems near R = 1 are maximally efficient (minimum thermodynamic waste)

## Conclusion

**Status:** Theoretical
**Bridge:** Thermodynamics ↔ n=6 ↔ arrow of time ↔ Landauer
