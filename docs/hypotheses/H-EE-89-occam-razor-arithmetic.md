- Occam's razor: among equivalent explanations, prefer the simplest
- Solomonoff-Levin universal prior: simpler theories have higher prior probability
- R(n) = sigma(n)*phi(n)/(n*tau(n)): five arithmetic functions, one equation
- R(6) = 1: the unique fixed point of this function over natural numbers
- K(R(6)=1) is minimal because: (a) all terms are standard number theory functions,
  (b) the result is the integer 1, (c) n=6 is the smallest solution
- Alternative statements of the same fact require more bits:
  "12*2 = 6*4" (7 symbols) vs "R(6)=1" (5 symbols)
- The Minimum Description Length (MDL) principle selects R(6)=1 as the canonical law
- Physical laws with K-complexity near minimal are discovered early (Kepler, Newton, Einstein)
