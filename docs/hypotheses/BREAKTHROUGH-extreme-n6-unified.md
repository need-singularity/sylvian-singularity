## 7. Honest Limitations

1. **Small number dominance**: n=6 functions produce {1,2,3,4,5,6,12}. These cover
   most small integers. The 57% hit rate, while far above random, partly reflects this.

2. **Post-hoc selection**: We searched FOR n=6 patterns. A controlled comparison
   against n=8, n=10, n=12 is needed. If they show similar rates, n=6 is not special.

3. **No mechanism**: We show CORRELATION, not CAUSATION. Why should a number-theoretic
   property (σ(n)=2n) dictate nuclear binding or Cooper pairing?

4. **Domain correlation**: ITER parameters appear in multiple domains (SCMAG, TOKAMAK,
   FENGR). True independence is lower than 190.

5. **Refuted predictions**: n/p ratio at BBN = 1/7 NOT 1/6. Fine structure constant
   has no clean n=6 expression. These failures constrain the theory's scope.

6. **Engineering contamination**: ~30% of hypotheses involve engineering choices
   (ITER design), not physics laws. Restricting to physics-only reduces the count
   but maintains significance (physics-only structural rate ~60%).
