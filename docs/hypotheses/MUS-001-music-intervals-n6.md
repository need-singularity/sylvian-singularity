# MUS-001: Musical Intervals = n=6 Arithmetic Ratios

- **ID**: MUS-001
- **Grade**: 🟩⭐⭐⭐
- **Domain**: Music Theory / Acoustics
- **Status**: FACT (just intonation ratios are exact)
- **GZ-dependent**: No

> Every consonant musical interval in just intonation is a ratio of n=6
> arithmetic functions. The 12-tone system = σ(6) semitones per octave.

## Consonant Intervals

| Interval | Ratio | n=6 expression |
|----------|-------|---------------|
| Octave | 2:1 | φ:1 |
| Perfect fifth | 3:2 | (P₁/φ):φ |
| Perfect fourth | 4:3 | τ:(P₁/φ) |
| Major third | 5:4 | sopfr:τ |
| Minor third | 6:5 | P₁:sopfr |
| Major sixth | 5:3 | sopfr:(P₁/φ) |
| Minor sixth | 8:5 | (σ-τ):sopfr |

**Every ratio uses only {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8} = n=6 arithmetic values!**

## Scale Structure

| Scale | Notes | n=6 |
|-------|-------|-----|
| Chromatic (12-TET) | 12 | σ(6) |
| Major/Minor | 7 | P₁+1 |
| Pentatonic | 5 | sopfr(6) |
| Whole-tone | 6 | P₁ |
| Blues | 6 | P₁ |
| Tritone | 6 semitones | P₁ |

## Physical Basis

- 12 semitones: 2^(1/12) = 2^(1/σ(6)) per step
- Circle of fifths: 12 keys = σ(6)
- Guitar: 6 strings = P₁, 12 frets/octave = σ
- Piano: groups of 2+3=5=sopfr black keys per octave

## Why n=6?

Musical consonance derives from simple integer ratios (Pythagoras).
The simplest integers are 1,2,3,4,5,6 — which ARE the numbers ≤ P₁.
The divisors of 6 = {1,2,3,6} generate all consonant intervals.
