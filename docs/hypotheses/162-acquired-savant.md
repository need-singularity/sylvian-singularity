## Background

Acquired savant syndrome is a phenomenon where individuals with no prior extraordinary abilities suddenly develop exceptional abilities (music, art, mathematics, calendar calculation, etc.) after brain injury (accident, stroke, disease). According to Treffert, there are dozens of confirmed cases worldwide.

This phenomenon is the most dramatic empirical evidence of "cusp transition" (Hypothesis 003) in our model. An individual in the normal range experiences a single brain injury event that rapidly changes parameters, causing a "step jump" into the Golden Zone.

Related hypotheses: Hypothesis 003 (cusp transition), Hypothesis 124 (step jump), Hypothesis 156 (Sylvian fissure), Hypothesis 161 (left-right brain)

## Limitations

- Very few acquired savant cases (< 100 cases), insufficient statistical power
- D, P, I values for each case are post-hoc estimates, not directly measured
- Almost no pre-accident baseline data
- Uncertain if all acquired savants share the same mechanism
- Timeline of compensatory rewiring (immediate vs gradual) varies by case
- Possible self-report bias
