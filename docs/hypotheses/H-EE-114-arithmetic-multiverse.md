## Physical Interpretation of R(n)

  R(n) < 1: Universe has more "holes" than substance.
    - Too few divisors relative to totient
    - Sparse, disconnected structures
    - Cannot sustain stable composite objects
    - Example: R(2) = 3/4 — mostly empty

  R(n) = 1: Perfect arithmetic balance.
    - Exactly as many ways to factor as ways to be coprime
    - Stable composite structures possible
    - Entropy and order in balance
    - Example: R(6) = 1 — our universe

  R(n) > 1: Universe has more structure than it can sustain.
    - Too many divisors relative to totient
    - Over-constrained, overfitted
    - Spontaneous symmetry breaking everywhere
    - Example: R(12) = 14/9 — over-structured, phase transitions everywhere

## Stability Argument

A universe with R = 1 is at a fixed point:
  - sigma measures total divisorial "energy"
  - phi measures "free" (coprime) degrees of freedom
  - tau measures discrete structure count
  - n measures scale

  Balance: energy = scale * structure
  Imbalance: runaway (R > 1) or collapse (R < 1)

Only at R = 1 does the universe neither explode (R > 1) nor collapse (R < 1).
