# H-EE-93: p-Adic Solutions to R(n) = 1

## Hypothesis

> The equation sigma(n)*phi(n) = n*tau(n) may admit additional solutions
> when interpreted in p-adic number fields Q_p, beyond the real solutions n=1 and n=6.
> Different p-adic fields could correspond to distinct "perfect architectures."

## Background

- Over the integers: sigma(n)*phi(n) = n*tau(n) has solutions n=1 and n=6
- p-adic numbers Q_p extend Q with a different notion of distance and convergence
- A number "small" in Q_p may be "large" in R and vice versa
- Arithmetic functions sigma, phi, tau extend naturally to p-adic integers

## The p-Adic Setting

In Q_p, the equation becomes:
  sigma_p(n) * phi_p(n) = n * tau_p(n)

where the functions are evaluated with p-adic absolute values.

For p=2: 2-adic integers include all integers, but convergence differs.
The 2-adic valuation v_2(6) = 1, v_2(28) = 2, suggesting the perfect
number sequence 6, 28, 496, ... has a natural 2-adic interpretation.

## Conjectured Structure

- Q_2: Additional solutions may exist among 2-adic integers not in Z
- Q_3: The factor of 3 in n=6 = 2*3 makes Q_3 natural; new solutions possible
- Q_p for p > 3: Likely no new solutions (6 has no factor of p)

## Interpretation for AI

If p-adic solutions exist, they would represent architectures that are
"balanced" under a different metric — optimal for hardware with p-adic
memory hierarchies or modular arithmetic units (e.g., FPGAs with binary trees).

## Conclusion

**Status: Open mathematics**
**Key question:** Does sigma(x)*phi(x) = x*tau(x) have p-adic solutions outside Z?
**Bridge:** p-adic architecture theory — different number systems, different optima.
