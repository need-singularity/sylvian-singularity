# H-EE-52: Bekenstein Bound Scaled by R(n)

## Hypothesis

> The maximum information content of a physical system is S <= (2*pi*E*R)/(hbar*c) * R(n), where R(n) is the architecture's balance ratio. Black holes saturate this bound at R=1: they are perfect R=1 information processors. Neural networks approach the bound as R approaches 1.

## Background

- Bekenstein bound: S <= 2*pi*R*E/(hbar*c) — max entropy in region of size R with energy E
- Black holes saturate the bound (holographic principle)
- Neural networks: information stored in parameters bounded by physical substrate
- R(n) scaling: efficiency of information storage depends on architectural balance
- At R=1: maximum information per unit energy (Bekenstein-saturating)

## Predictions

1. Information capacity per parameter is maximized at R=1 architectures
2. R<1 architectures store less information per unit energy (sub-Bekenstein)
3. The ratio (actual info)/(Bekenstein limit) ~ R(architecture)
4. This provides a physical upper bound on neural network capacity

## Conclusion

**Status:** Theoretical — connects information physics to architecture design
**Bridge:** Black hole physics ↔ information theory ↔ R(n)
