## Background

### The Arrow of Time Problem

The basic laws of physics are time-symmetric. Yet macroscopically, time flows in only one direction:
- **Thermodynamic arrow**: Entropy S always increases (dS/dt вүҘ 0)
- **Cosmological arrow**: The universe expands (dR/dt > 0)
- **Psychological arrow**: We remember the past and predict the future

Why does time flow in only one direction? вҖ” This is one of the fundamental mysteries of physics.

### The Model's Convergence Direction

In meta-iteration, I always converges toward I*=1/3:
- I > I*: I decreases (dI/dt < 0)
- I = I*: Fixed point (dI/dt = 0)
- I < I*: I increases (dI/dt > 0, rebounds from lower bound)

Since I > I* for most physical initial conditions, **I proceeds in the decreasing direction**.

## Interpretation

From the interpretation that the arrow of time = direction of meta-convergence:

1. **Explaining the asymmetry of time** вҖ” Physical laws are time-symmetric, but meta-iteration has an attractor (I*), so asymmetry is natural. The direction of time = direction of convergence toward the attractor
2. **Reinterpretation of entropy** вҖ” Entropy increase is another expression of "inhibition decrease." More accessible microstates = inhibition released
3. **Meaning of heat death** вҖ” Heat death (S вҶ’ S_max) corresponds to I вҶ’ I* (fixed point) = all change stops = meta-iteration convergence complete
4. **Uniqueness of the Big Bang** вҖ” Low entropy at the Big Bang (S вүҲ 0) corresponds to I вҶ’ вҲһ (maximum inhibition). "Starts from a state where everything is inhibited"

Combined with Hypothesis 151 (inflation): inflation is the period where I decreases rapidly = period where entropy increases rapidly = period where time "flows quickly."
