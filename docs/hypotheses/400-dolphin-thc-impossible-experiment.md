## Limitations

1. **Dolphin THC exposure is unethical and untestable.** All dolphin+THC
   predictions are purely model-derived. Accidental exposure (e.g., wastewater
   cannabis contamination near dolphin habitats) has been reported in media but
   not rigorously documented in scientific literature.

2. **Human baseline (α, β) = (0.65, 0.35) is a model estimate**, not measured.
   Individual variation is large; the model predicts population-level tendencies
   not individual responses.

3. **TTX human microdosing ethics.** The proposed TTX circle protocol requires
   medical ethics approval, precise dosing infrastructure, and emergency
   protocols for adverse reactions. The therapeutic window for TTX is narrow.

4. **The synchrony bonus is not yet formalized.** The claim that circle ritual
   adds a coherence term independent of (α, β) is a new mechanism proposed
   here, not derived from prior hypotheses. It requires independent verification
   (EEG inter-brain synchrony measurement).

5. **Golden Zone dependency.** The (α, β) parameterization and the variational
   principle (minimize distance to diagonal) are GZ-dependent. The navigation
   model assumes GZ correctness; if GZ is refuted, the quantitative predictions
   collapse, though the qualitative species-substance matching observation remains.

6. **The evolutionary co-evolution claim** (receptor adaptation over generations)
   is a standard evolutionary biology hypothesis, but specific genetic evidence
   for the predicted variants has not been gathered. This is a testable
   prediction for genomic studies.
