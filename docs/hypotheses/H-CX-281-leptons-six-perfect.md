# H-CX-281: ⭐⭐⭐🟩 6 Types of Leptons = P₁ = Perfect Number!

> **Hypothesis**: The number of lepton species in the Standard Model equals the first perfect number P₁ = 6, independently mirroring the quark sector (H-CX-280).

## Background

The Standard Model contains exactly 6 leptons: electron (e), muon (mu), tau (tau), and their corresponding neutrinos (nu_e, nu_mu, nu_tau). Like quarks, they are arranged in 3 generations of 2 particles each. The fact that both quark and lepton sectors independently contain P1 = 6 members is required by anomaly cancellation in electroweak theory.

Anomaly cancellation (the condition that quantum gauge symmetries remain consistent) requires:
- Number of lepton doublets = Number of quark doublets (times number of colors)
- This constraint links N_leptons = N_quarks = 6

## Deep Structure: Quark-Lepton Symmetry

The quark-lepton symmetry N_q = N_l = P1 is not arbitrary. In the Standard Model:
- SU(2)_L anomaly cancellation: sum of weak isospins = 0
- This requires each generation to have 1 lepton doublet + 3 quark doublets (3 colors)
- The factor 3 = P1/phi(P1) appears in both anomaly cancellation and divisor arithmetic

## Limitations

- Anomaly cancellation determines N_generations, not directly N_leptons
- Right-handed neutrinos (if they exist) would add 3 more, making 9 total
- The hypothesis counts only Standard Model leptons; BSM particles could change the count
