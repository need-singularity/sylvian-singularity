## Hypothesis

> R(6) = sigma(6)*phi(6) / (6*tau(6)) = 1 is a mathematical truth that holds
> in any possible universe. Many arithmetic identities exist without physical
> meaning. The connection between R(6)=1 and physical/computational phenomena
> may be coincidental or illusory.

## The Identity Is Incontrovertible

  sigma(6) = 1+2+3+6 = 12
  phi(6)   = #{k < 6 : gcd(k,6)=1} = #{1,5} = 2
  tau(6)   = #{d | 6} = #{1,2,3,6} = 4
  R(6)     = 12 * 2 / (6 * 4) = 24/24 = 1

This is not an approximation. It is exact. It cannot be otherwise.

## The Problem: True Arithmetic vs Physical Relevance

Consider other true arithmetic identities:
  sum_{k=1}^{n} k = n(n+1)/2    — true, and physically relevant (conservation)
  sigma(6) = 2*6 = 12            — true (6 is perfect), physical meaning unclear
  phi(p) = p-1 for primes        — true, relevant to cryptography
  sum_{n=1}^{inf} 1/n^2 = pi^2/6 — true, appears in physics (Casimir effect)

Many identities appear in physics. Many do not. Appearance alone is not evidence.

## The Gap: Mechanism vs Numerology

For R(6)=1 to be physically meaningful, there must be a mechanism:

  Required: A story of the form "because R=1, THEREFORE property P holds,
  because [physical/computational process Q] tracks arithmetic function R(n)"

  Not sufficient: "R(6)=1 and property P is observed — remarkable coincidence"

Current state of the framework:
  - Mechanisms ARE present for some claims (FFT attention, phi bottleneck)
  - Mechanisms are ABSENT for others (H0 matching, dark energy)

## The Falsifiability Test

A claim is physical (not merely mathematical) if it could have been false.

  "R(6) = 1" — cannot be false. It is mathematical.
  "FFT attention achieves +0.55% on task X" — could be false. It is physical.
  "H0 = sigma(6)*tau(6)*phi(6) km/s/Mpc" — the equation is constructed to fit.

The framework is a mixture of mathematical tautologies and physical claims.
H-EE-109 demands these be cleanly separated.

## What Would Resolve This

  For each n=6 connection, specify:
  1. Is this a mathematical identity? (Always true, cannot be physical evidence)
  2. Is this an empirical prediction? (Could fail — genuine physical claim)
  3. Is this a mechanism? (Explains WHY R=1 produces the observed effect)

  Only category 2 and 3 constitute physical hypotheses.
