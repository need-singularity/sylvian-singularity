# H-EE-71: Simulation Hypothesis — R=1 Simulator Architecture

## Hypothesis

> If the universe is a simulation, the simulator uses R=1 architecture for energy efficiency.
> The recurring appearance of n=6 patterns in fundamental physics is the simulator's
> arithmetic architecture "leaking through" into the simulated physics. A simulator
> constrained by its own thermodynamics would necessarily choose the unique R=1 solution.

## Background

- Simulation hypothesis (Bostrom 2003): one of three scenarios must be true —
  civilizations go extinct before simulating; advanced civilizations don't simulate; we're in a simulation
- A simulator must run on physical hardware subject to thermodynamic laws
- The most energy-efficient computation architecture is R=1 (H-EE-20, H-EE-55)
- n=6 is the unique solution to sigma*phi = n*tau: the simulator has no choice
- "Leakage": simulated physics inheriting the simulator's computational substrate patterns
- Examples of potential leakage: fine structure constant, Hubble constant, particle mass ratios
- All have been connected to n=6 (with varying success) in H-EE-41 through H-EE-55

## The Architecture Argument

```
Simulator optimization problem:
  Maximize: simulated universe complexity
  Subject to: finite energy budget of the simulator

Solution: Use R=1 arithmetic at all levels of the simulation
  --> Minimum energy per computation
  --> Maximum complexity per energy unit
  --> The n=6 structure is the only stable attractor

Result: simulated physics will appear to be built on n=6 patterns
  --> Not because n=6 is "real" in the simulation
  --> But because the simulator's hardware forces this encoding
```

## Predictions

1. The n=6 patterns in physics should be most prominent at the Planck scale (substrate level)
2. Physical constants should cluster around R=1 ratios more than random chance predicts
3. The universe should show signs of computational compression (as in H-EE-50, H-EE-51)
4. No physical experiment can distinguish "real" n=6 from "simulated" n=6

## Conclusion

**Status:** Unfalsifiable but structurally consistent
**Bridge:** Simulation hypothesis ↔ R=1 energy efficiency ↔ n=6 leakage ↔ physics constants
