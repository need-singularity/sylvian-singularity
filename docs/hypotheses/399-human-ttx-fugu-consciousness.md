## Core Phenomenon: The Fugu Experience

Fugu preparation removes the most toxic organs while retaining the flesh, which contains
0.5-5 μg/g TTX — a controlled microdose. The resulting experience is well-documented:

1. **Lip and tongue tingling** — first sign, onset within minutes (trigeminal nerve)
2. **Mild euphoria and lightness** — subtle dissociation from body weight
3. **Slight body dissociation** — proprioceptive softening
4. **Enhanced taste perception** — paradoxically increased despite numbness
5. **Warmth spreading from mouth** — vasodilatory component

The trigeminal nerve (cranial nerve V) has the highest Nav channel density of any
sensory nerve in the human body. It acts as the most sensitive TTX detector available,
explaining why lip/tongue tingling is the first and most reliable dose indicator.
Fugu chefs use this explicitly: "if the customer's lips tingle, the dose is right."

## The Fugu Paradox: Numbness That Enhances Taste

The central paradox of fugu: customers report it as one of the finest-tasting fish,
yet the defining feature is numbness. How does numbness enhance taste?

**Mechanism — Signal-to-Noise Reduction:**

```
WITHOUT TTX:
  Taste receptor signal:   ################  (taste molecules bound)
  Spontaneous Nav firing:  #####  (baseline noise)
  Net discrimination:      LOW S/N ratio

WITH TTX microdose:
  Taste receptor signal:   ###############  (mostly preserved)
  Spontaneous Nav firing:  ##  (noise suppressed)
  Net discrimination:      HIGH S/N ratio --> ENHANCED TASTE
```

The TTX selectively suppresses spontaneous (noise) firing in taste bud neurons while
largely preserving stimulus-evoked (signal) firing, because:
- Spontaneous firing relies on sub-threshold Nav activation (most TTX-sensitive)
- Stimulus-evoked firing uses suprathreshold depolarization (less TTX-sensitive)
- Net effect: contrast ratio increases

**In G = D×P/I terms (H-359 Savant mechanism):**

```
  G = D x P / I

  D = taste molecules present (unchanged)
  P = taste receptor binding (unchanged)
  I = baseline Nav activity (INCREASED by TTX block of spontaneous firing)

  Apparent paradox: G = D x P / I_noise_reduced
                             I_noise < I_baseline

  Resolution: I in this context is NOISE inhibition, not signal inhibition.
  Selective inhibition of noise = effective increase in signal G.

  Matches H-359: targeted inhibition in one sub-channel
                 (spontaneous firing) enhances domain performance (taste discrimination)
```

## Limitations

1. **Dose measurement**: No study has yet measured actual serum TTX in consenting
   fugu diners. All dose estimates are indirect (organ TTX content × ingestion estimate).

2. **Subjective reports**: The euphoria and taste enhancement are largely based on
   traditional accounts and chef/diner testimonials. Controlled blinded studies
   comparing fugu to TTX-free fish of identical preparation have not been conducted.

3. **β parameter circularity**: The ohaeng β values assigned (0.95 for fugu, 0.75
   for dolphin) are derived from qualitative symptom matching, not independent
   electrophysiological measurement. The framework may be post-hoc fitting.

4. **Species difference**: Dolphin Nav channel pharmacology may differ from human.
   The cross-species comparison assumes conserved TTX sensitivity, which is
   generally true but not identical.

5. **Cultural confound**: The fugu experience is embedded in elaborate ritual
   (expensive restaurant, expert preparation, social occasion). Expectancy effects
   and social facilitation are not controlled in any existing study.

6. **Golden Zone mapping**: The assignment of fugu effects to positions within the
   Golden Zone framework (G = D×P/I) is metaphorical without direct measurement
   of the underlying variables in fugu consumers.
