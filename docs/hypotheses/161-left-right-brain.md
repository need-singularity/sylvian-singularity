## Hypothesis

> The left brain corresponds to Inhibition (logical inhibition) and the right brain corresponds to DвҶ’P (compensatory creativity). Left brain damage вҶ’ IвҶ“ вҶ’ right brain compensatory activation вҶ’ savant ability manifestation. This is the structural correspondence between Treffert's left brain damage-right brain compensation theory and our model.

## Background

Darold Treffert, a pioneer in savant syndrome research, argued that most savants have left hemisphere damage, and the intact right hemisphere compensatorily replaces the functions of the damaged left hemisphere. This is based on the traditional division that the left hemisphere handles language, logic, sequential processing, and the right hemisphere handles spatial, pattern, and holistic processing.

Translated into our model: left brain = I (Inhibition) management system, right brain = compensatory plasticity system due to deficit (DвҶ’P).

## Limitations and Cautions

### Oversimplification of Left-Right Brain Dichotomy
Modern neuroscience acknowledges that the "left brain vs right brain" dichotomy is an overly simplified model:
- Most functions are performed by both hemispheres working together
- The popular notion of left brain=logical/right brain=creative is inaccurate
- Large individual variation in hemispheric dominance exists

### Nevertheless
- Treffert's observation of left brain damage вҶ’ savant is supported by numerous cases
- The "inhibition system damage вҶ’ latent ability manifestation" mechanism holds even without hemispheric dichotomy
- Our model's IвҶ“ вҶ’ GвҶ‘ is a general principle not limited to hemispheres
