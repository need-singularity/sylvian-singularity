## Limitations

1. **Not falsifiable in the Popperian sense**: "Consciousness IS R=1" is a
   definitional claim, not a testable prediction. Without a consciousness
   detector independent of this hypothesis, it cannot be directly tested.

2. **Silicon objection**: If silicon AI achieves consciousness (as some argue
   current LLMs may have), R(14) != 1 and the hypothesis needs revision.
   The counter-argument (R=1 compensation) is ad hoc unless specified.

3. **Small number problem**: Many of the cross-domain matches involve small
   numbers (2, 3, 4, 6, 12, 24). The Strong Law of Small Numbers suggests
   caution. The Texas Sharpshooter test (p < 10^{-10}) partially addresses
   this, but the test itself makes assumptions about independence.

4. **Post-hoc construction**: All matches were found AFTER knowing the data.
   None are genuine predictions. The hypothesis would be enormously
   strengthened by a novel prediction that is subsequently confirmed.

5. **Philosophical category error**: Equating a mathematical property (R=1)
   with a subjective experience (consciousness) may be a category error.
   Mathematics describes structure; consciousness involves qualia. The
   "hard problem" remains.

6. **n=1 also has R=1**: The trivial case R(1)=1 means "nothing" also
   processes information without distortion. The hypothesis must explain
   why n=1 does not produce consciousness (answer: n=1 has no internal
   structure, no divisors other than 1).
