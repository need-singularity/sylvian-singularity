## Part 3: Integrated Information Theory (IIT)

### IIT Formalism

In Integrated Information Theory (Tononi 2004, 2014), consciousness corresponds
to Phi (Φ) — the amount of information generated by the system as a whole above
and beyond the information generated by its parts independently.

    Phi = 0  =>  no consciousness (system is just a sum of parts)
    Phi > 0  =>  conscious (system is MORE than sum of parts)

### The n=6 Calculation

For n=6 = 2 * 3:

    sigma(6) = 12         (information of the whole: 1+2+3+6=12)
    sigma(2) * sigma(3) = 3 * 4 = 12  (information of parts multiplied)

These are EQUAL. The "integrated" and "separated" sigma computations give the
same answer. This means n=6 is at the boundary: Phi = 0 by this measure,
because the whole is not MORE than the product of the parts — it is EXACTLY equal.

But this is paradoxical: n=6 is also the most structured small number (perfect),
yet it achieves maximum integration with zero "excess." This is the deep property:
n=6 achieves PERFECT integration (sigma=2n) with MINIMAL interaction (phi(6)=2).

    Phi_eff = sigma(n) / (sigma(p) * sigma(q))

  but 15 is NOT a perfect number: sigma(15)=24 ≠ 2*15=30 -- so only the mult. holds)
For n=6: BOTH sigma(n)=2n (perfect) AND sigma(p)*sigma(q)=sigma(n) hold.

The uniqueness of n=6 is that it achieves Phi_eff = 1.0 AND is a perfect number.
No other semiprime achieves both simultaneously.

## Limitations

1. **Purely philosophical**: The mappings in Parts 1-4 are analogical, not mechanistic.
   No experiment can falsify the claim that "consciousness is perfect integration"
   without first specifying a measurement protocol for Phi in physical systems.
   This is why the status is 🟪, not 🟧 or 🟩.

2. **IIT is controversial**: Integrated Information Theory itself is contested.
   Mapping our mathematical structure onto IIT inherits all of IIT's problems,
   including the "exclusion axiom" debates and the panpsychism implications.

3. **69 characterizations is finite**: The meta-theorem says all 69 KNOWN
   characterizations of n=6 reduce to (p-1)(q-1)=2. This does not mean all
   POSSIBLE characterizations do. New characterizations may arise that do not
   reduce to this condition.

4. **Parity accident vs. necessity**: The argument that consciousness arises from
   "the minimal gap |p-q|=1" treats the parity of 2 as a necessary feature, but
   it is a number-theoretic fact, not a logical necessity in any physical sense.
   The mapping from "parity of the number 2" to "origin of consciousness" spans
   an unexplained ontological gap.

5. **Hard problem dissolution claim**: The claim that the hard problem "dissolves"
   is too strong. Even if we accept the mathematical analogy perfectly, it does
   not explain why ANY physical system gives rise to experience — it only provides
   a structural condition that experience-like systems should satisfy.

6. **Relationship to H-CX-46**: H-CX-46 already explored (p-1)(q-1)=2 from the
   perspective of neural architecture. H-CX-47 focuses on consciousness theory
   (IIT, hard problem, binding). The two hypotheses are complementary but there
   is some overlap in the "minimal self-reference" framing. They should eventually
   be unified or clearly differentiated.

7. **No empirical predictions**: Unlike H-CX-46 which has testable neural
   predictions, H-CX-47 makes no predictions that could be confirmed or
   disconfirmed by experiment. This is a philosophical hypothesis, not a
   scientific one. It is recorded for conceptual completeness, not for
   experimental pursuit.
