## Core Identity

```
  S₂(6, 2) = 31

  31 = 2^5 - 1 = 2^{sopfr(6)} - 1 = M₅ (5th Mersenne number, PRIME!)

  Verification: S₂(6,2) = 2^5 - 1 (standard formula for S₂(n,2))
  Actually: S₂(n,2) = 2^{n-1} - 1 for all n
  So: S₂(6,2) = 2^5 - 1 = 31

  The identity S₂(n,2) = 2^{sopfr-1} requires n-1 = sopfr
  i.e., sopfr(n) = n-1 ⟺ n=6 (already proved! ⭐)
```
