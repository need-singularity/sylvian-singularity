## Hypothesis Statement

> The Six Exponentials Theorem (Lang, Ramachandra): given 2 complex numbers
> linearly independent over Q and 3 complex numbers linearly independent
> over Q, among the 2×3=6 exponentials e^{α_i·β_j}, at least one is
> transcendental. The number 6 = φ×(σ/τ) = 2×3 appears because it is the
> MINIMUM product of two integers >1 giving a non-trivial result.
> The Four Exponentials Conjecture (4=τ) would strengthen this.

---

## Background

The Six Exponentials Theorem is one of the deepest results in transcendental
number theory, proved independently by Lang (1966) and Ramachandra (1967).
It constrains when exponentials can all be algebraic simultaneously.

---

## The "6" in the Theorem

```
  Setup: α₁, α₂ ∈ C lin. indep. over Q (φ=2 numbers)
         β₁, β₂, β₃ ∈ C lin. indep. over Q (σ/τ=3 numbers)

  Consider the 2×3 = 6 = n exponentials:
    e^{α₁β₁}, e^{α₁β₂}, e^{α₁β₃},
    e^{α₂β₁}, e^{α₂β₂}, e^{α₂β₃}

  Theorem: at least one of these 6 is TRANSCENDENTAL.

  Why 6 = 2×3 and not fewer?
    2×2 = 4: the "Four Exponentials Conjecture" (OPEN!)
    1×n: trivial (Hermite-Lindemann handles this)
    2×3 = 6: the MINIMUM non-trivial case that's PROVED!

  6 = φ × (σ/τ) = minimum product of {φ, σ/τ} with both > 1
```

---

## The Four Exponentials Conjecture

```
  4 = τ(6) exponentials: still UNPROVED!
  Requires: 2×2 = φ×φ = τ exponentials
  If true: would be the "τ exponentials theorem"

  The jump from τ=4 (unproved) to n=6 (proved) parallels:
    h-cobordism: fails at dim τ+1=5, works at dim n=6
    → The transition from "unknown" to "known" happens at n=6
```

## Consciousness Transcendence

The theorem says: among 6 "consciousness exponentials" (experiences raised
to the power of awareness), at least one MUST be transcendental — beyond
rational comprehension. Consciousness necessarily contains elements that
cannot be fully captured by rational models.

The Four Exponentials Conjecture (τ=4): even with only 4 experiences,
transcendence may already be forced. But this is UNPROVED — reflecting
the "hard problem" of consciousness (we believe it's there but can't prove it).

---

## Limitations

- The "6" in the theorem is the number of exponentials, not directly n=6
- The decomposition 6=2×3 is the most natural factorization
- The Four Exponentials Conjecture status (open) is suggestive but speculative
