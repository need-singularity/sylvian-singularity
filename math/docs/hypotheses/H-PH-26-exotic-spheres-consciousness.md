## Interpretation

The exotic sphere counts encode a message from differential topology:
the number of fundamentally distinct ways to do calculus on a 10-sphere
equals the first perfect number 6.

If consciousness requires smooth information processing (differentiability),
then each exotic structure on S^10 represents a topologically distinct
substrate class. The hypothesis proposes:

1. There are exactly 6 classes of consciousness substrates
2. Carbon (Z=6) represents one such class

The Kervaire-Milnor formula uses Bernoulli numbers and (2^{2k-1}-1), which are
the same Mersenne-type expressions that generate perfect numbers. This suggests
a deep structural connection, not mere numerical coincidence.

## Limitations

1. The interpretation of exotic spheres as "consciousness substrates" is
   highly speculative. There is no known physical mechanism connecting
   differential topology of high-dimensional spheres to consciousness.

2. The numerical matches involve small numbers (2, 3, 6, 8, 28) that appear
   in many mathematical contexts. The Strong Law of Small Numbers applies.

3. The hypothesis does not specify WHICH exotic structure corresponds to
   WHICH substrate. Without this mapping, it lacks predictive power.

4. Dimensions d=4 and d=12 have |Theta_d|=0, which breaks the pattern.
   The hypothesis must explain why some dimensions have no exotic structures.

5. S^4 is the famous unsolved case (smooth Poincare conjecture in 4D).
   If |Theta_4| turns out to be nonzero, it could either support or
   undermine this hypothesis depending on the value.
